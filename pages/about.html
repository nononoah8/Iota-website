ABOUT US

Iota Omega Epsilon is a co-ed professional fraternity at the University of Michigan, operating at the intersection of business and STEM, dedicated to shaping the innovators of tomorrow. We aim to foster a brotherhood of like-minded individuals committed to optimizing the engineering and business sectors. We hope to support our members in advancing their academic and professional goals within and beyond the realms of STEM and business.

Founded at the University of Michigan in 2023, our organization is built on four pillars: philanthropy, ambition, development, and community. Above all, this fraternity and its membership strive to create fellowship through a fraternal bond between members, providing them with personal experiences to aid in their pursuit of academic and professional development.

Our Pillars

Ambition

Our members are extremely ambitious, always searching for new ways to expand their knowledge and experience base. Through professional and academic development opportunities, Iota Omega Epsilon members have ample opportunities to grow within a community of driven students.

Philanthropy

Our philanthropy drives impactful change within our community. Through financial contributions, volunteerism, and mentorship, we empower future leaders and address pressing societal challenges. Together, we embody the ethos of giving back, catalyzing lasting positive change.

Community

At Iota Omega Epsilon, our Community is the heart of our fraternity. We are dedicated to building a tight-knit brotherhood where members support, inspire, and collaborate. Through mentorship, networking, and community service, we foster inclusivity as well as social and professional growth.

Development

The development pillar of Iota Omega Epsilon is distinguished by innovative initiatives aimed at empowering members both professionally and socially. With a commitment to continuous evolution, Iota ensures its members are equipped with the skills and connections necessary to excel in the intersection between business and STEM.
